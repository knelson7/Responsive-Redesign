Responsive Redesign Project

Project Outline

The goal of this project was to practice the workflow of redesigning a simple website. Through this project, I gained the skills necessary to analyze and identify flaws in an existing interface, create low-fidelity and high-fidelity prototypes for various screen sizes, and build a responsive website based on those prototypes.

Chosen Website: sanmateo.org

This website is dedicated to San Mateo County citizens as a way of informing them of current events, providing them with helpfpul resources, and harboring a strong sense of community.

Interface Problems

  • Typeface: the typeface is legible, but the random and inconsistent differences in weighting and size is distracting and doesn’t inform the hierarchy
  • Structure and Layout: there is not structure to the website besides the table. Pieces of information are slapped onto the page with no underlying foundation, structure, or order
  • White Space: white space is not used effectively with some sections of the webpage displaying dense amounts of text while other sections have little to no text
  • Color: the use of color is sporadic, random, and inconsistent. The green and yellow displayed on the page doesn’t help the reader’s eyes focus on important pieces of information
  • Other notes: there are duplicate pieces of information (such as the link to San Mateo County’s official website), the random urls outside of the main table throw off any organization that the table tries to provide

Accessibility Concerns

WebAIM WAVE detected many possible accessibility concerns including the following: the language of the document wasn’t identified, the page has no headings, no page regions or ARIA landmarks were found, and the contrast between the green text and the background color was very low. Lastly it found that the link text contained extraneous text that may not make sense out of context.

When using NVDA on the website, it would sometimes say “blank” instead of reading the next piece of text on the page. This is probably due to the website’s poor layout and structural issues. The screen reader interpreted the table of hyperlinks quite well, correctly notifying users when it switched to the next column.

Finished Product: Redesigned Website

This website is dedicated to San Mateo County citizens as a way of informing them of current events, providing them with helpfpul resources, and harboring a strong sense of community.

My redesigned website changes based on the size of the browser window. Most notably, the picture of San Mateo County City Hall gets bigger or smaller to fill up the correct amount of available space, while still displaying the full image. Additionally, the number of columns in the flexbox changes. On a full sized laptop screen, the browser shows 4 columns, with an iPad sized screen showing 2 columns, and an iPhone screen showing only a single column. When I changed the font size of my browser window to be as large as possible, it showed 3 columns. My website's improvements were highlihgted by changing the langauge of the website since the new structure and heirarchies that I put in place greatly inform where the user can click and what content is grouped together.
